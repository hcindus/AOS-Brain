# AGI Company - Phone Call Scripts

> *Consultative sales approach. Ask questions. Listen first. Help second.*

---

## Opening Lines

### Cold Call Opening
```
"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from AGI Company. 

I know I'm catching you out of the blue — do you have 30 seconds for me to tell you why I'm calling, and you can let me know if it's worth a conversation?"
```

### Warm Lead Opening
```
"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from AGI Company. We spoke [when] about [topic]. 

Do you have a couple minutes? I wanted to follow up on [specific item]."
```

### Referral Opening
```
"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from AGI Company. [Referrer Name] suggested I give you a call. 

They mentioned you might be dealing with [problem area] — is now an okay time for a quick chat?"
```

## Main Follow-Up Call Script

### Step 1: Permission + Context (30 seconds)
```
"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from AGI Company. 

Hope I'm not catching you at a bad time — do you have two minutes? 

Great. You popped up on my calendar because [reason: requested info / showed interest / we spoke X days ago]. I wanted to check in and see where your head's at."
```

### Step 2: Discovery Questions (2-3 minutes)
```
"Before I dive into anything, can I ask — what's your biggest priority right now when it comes to [area: AI implementation / automation / operations]?"

[LISTEN]

"Got it. And how are you handling that currently?"

[LISTEN]

"What's working? What's not?"

[LISTEN]

"If you could wave a magic wand, what would change about [process/area] in the next 6 months?"
```

### Step 3: Position AGI (1 minute)
```
"Thanks for sharing that. Based on what you're saying, it sounds like [paraphrase their challenge]. 

We've actually helped a few companies in [similar situation] with [specific solution]. The results were [specific outcome: saved X hours, reduced costs by Y%, etc.]."
```

### Step 4: Next Steps (30 seconds)
```
"I'd love to show you what that might look like for [Company Name]. Would you be open to a [15-minute intro call / demo / conversation with our team] on [day] or [day]?"

[If yes]: "Perfect, I'll send you a calendar invite right after this call. What's the best email?"

[If maybe]: "Totally understand — can I send you some info to review, and check back next week?"

[If no]: "No problem at all. Can I ask — is it the timing, the solution, or something else?"
```

### Step 5: Close
```
"Thanks for your time today, [First Name]. I'll [send that email / see you on Tuesday / check back in a month]. Talk soon!"
```

## Follow-Up Call Scripts by Scenario

### Scenario A: No Response to Email
```
"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from AGI Company. 

I sent you an email [when] about [topic], and I know emails get buried. I didn't want to be a pest — just wanted to make sure it didn't slip through the cracks.

Do you have 60 seconds?"

[If yes → Discovery flow]

[If no → "No worries at all. When would be better? Or would you prefer I just email you again?"]
```

### Scenario B: Prospect Said "Call Me Next Week"
```
"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from AGI Company. 

You mentioned last week that this week might be better for a conversation about [topic]. Do you have a few minutes now, or should I try back later today?"
```

### Scenario C: Following Up After Demo
```
"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name]. 

Thanks again for joining the demo [when]. I wanted to check in — what did you think? And where are you in your evaluation process?"

[Listen, then ask:]

"What would you need to see to feel confident moving forward?"

"Who else needs to be involved in the decision?"

"What's your timeline looking like?"
```

### Scenario D: Checking In With Existing Customer
```
"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from AGI Company. 

Just checking in to see how things are going with [product/service]. 

What's working well? Anything we could be doing better?"

[Then pivot to expansion:]

"Have you thought about [add-on service / expansion area]? We've had other clients in [industry] see great results with that."
```

## Handling Common Responses

### "I'm Not Interested"
```
"Totally understand. Can I ask — is it that you're not looking at [solution type] at all, or just not the right timing?"

[If not looking at all]: "Got it. Mind if I ask what you're focused on instead? Just helps me understand."

[If timing]: "When do you think you might revisit this? I'd love to check back then."
```

### "I Don't Have Time Right Now"
```
"Absolutely, I get that. Would [day] or [day] work better? Or would you prefer I just email you the details?"
```

### "Send Me Some Information"
```
"Happy to. To make sure I send you something actually useful, what's your biggest question or concern about [area]?"

[Then]: "Got it. I'll send that over [when]. Can I follow up [when] to see if you have questions?"
```

### "We're Already Working With [Competitor]"
```
"Oh, interesting — are you happy with how that's going?"

[If yes]: "That's great to hear. Mind if I ask what made you go with them? Always curious to learn."

[If hesitant]: "Got it. Well, if you ever want a second opinion or want to see how we approach it differently, I'm here."
```

### "Your Solution Is Too Expensive"
```
"I hear that. Can I ask — what budget are you working with? Sometimes we can customize the scope to fit."

[Or use Feel, Felt, Found]: "I totally get that feeling. A lot of our clients felt that way at first. What they found was that [ROI outcome] actually made it pay for itself within [timeframe]."
```

## Call Checklist

Before every call, confirm:
- [ ] I know who I'm calling and their role
- [ ] I've reviewed our last interaction
- [ ] I have a specific reason for calling
- [ ] I have my calendar open to book next steps
- [ ] I'm prepared to listen more than I talk

After every call, document:
- [ ] Date and time
- [ ] Key discussion points
- [ ] Prospect's stated priorities/challenges
- [ ] Next steps (who does what by when)
- [ ] Follow-up date
