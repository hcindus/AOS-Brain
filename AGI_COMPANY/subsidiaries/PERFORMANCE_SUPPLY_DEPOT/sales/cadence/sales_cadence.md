# AGI Company - Sales Cadence

> *The art of persistent, professional follow-up. Be present, not pushy.*

---

## What Is a Sales Cadence?

A sales cadence is a scheduled sequence of touchpoints with a prospect — the timing, channel, and message of each interaction. The goal is to stay top-of-mind while respecting the prospect's time and decision-making process.

**Our Philosophy:**
- Be helpful, not annoying
- Add value at every touchpoint
- Give prospects an easy out
- Adapt based on engagement signals

## Standard Cadence: New Inbound Lead

*For leads who have requested information or shown interest*

### Day 0 (Immediate)
- **Channel:** Email
- **Message:** Personalized welcome + resources promised
- **Goal:** Confirm receipt, set expectations
- **Template:** Initial Response

### Day 1
- **Channel:** Phone Call
- **Message:** Quick check-in to see if they have questions
- **Goal:** Build rapport, gauge urgency
- **Script:** Warm Lead Opening

### Day 3
- **Channel:** Email
- **Message:** Value-add content (case study, relevant article)
- **Goal:** Stay helpful, no hard ask
- **Template:** Interested Follow-Up

### Day 7
- **Channel:** Phone Call + Voicemail
- **Message:** Check in, offer to answer questions
- **Goal:** Move conversation forward
- **Script:** Main Follow-Up Call

### Day 10
- **Channel:** Email
- **Message:** Social proof (testimonial, case study results)
- **Goal:** Build credibility
- **Template:** Show Value

### Day 14
- **Channel:** LinkedIn (if connected) or Email
- **Message:** Brief, casual check-in
- **Goal:** Different channel, lower pressure
- **Template:** Casual Touch

### Day 21
- **Channel:** Phone Call
- **Message:** "Should I check back later?"
- **Goal:** Get clarity on timeline or close the loop
- **Script:** Timeline Check

### Day 28
- **Channel:** Email
- **Message:** Breakup email or nurture content
- **Goal:** Final attempt or move to nurture
- **Template:** Breakup Email

## Nurture Cadence: Long-Term Prospects

*For leads not ready to buy now but may be in the future*

### Monthly Rhythm
- **Week 1:** Email — valuable content (industry insight, case study, tip)
- **Week 3:** LinkedIn — engage with their content, share relevant post

### Quarterly Rhythm
- **Month 3:** Phone call — "Checking in, anything changed?"
- **Month 6:** Email — product updates, new features
- **Month 9:** Phone call — "Still on your radar?"
- **Month 12:** Email — annual check-in, industry trends

## Response-Based Adjustments

### If Prospect Responds Positively
- Accelerate the cadence
- Move to next stage immediately
- Offer next steps within 24-48 hours

### If Prospect Responds Negatively
- Acknowledge and respect their position
- Ask if you can follow up in the future
- Add to nurture list with longer intervals

### If Prospect Engages But Doesn't Respond
- Track email opens, link clicks, document views
- Use engagement signals to time next outreach
- Reference their engagement: "I saw you checked out [resource]..."

### If No Response After Full Cadence
- Send breakup email
- Move to long-term nurture (quarterly)
- Tag for future re-engagement campaigns
- Mark as "Closed - No Response" in CRM

## Cadence Best Practices

### Timing
- ✅ Space touchpoints 2-5 days apart (not daily)
- ✅ Avoid weekends (unless industry-appropriate)
- ✅ Time calls for late morning or early afternoon
- ✅ Test email send times for your audience

### Messaging
- ✅ Vary the value prop angle with each touch
- ✅ Lead with them, not with you
- ✅ Ask questions, don't just pitch
- ✅ Give them an easy way to respond or opt out

### Persistence
- ✅ 6-8 touchpoints before considering a break
- ✅ Always try multiple channels
- ✅ Track everything in CRM
- ✅ Respect their preferences if they share them

## Quick Reference: When to Stop

Stop the active cadence when:
- ❌ Prospect explicitly asks you to stop
- ❌ Prospect says "not interested" after you've tried "Feel, Felt, Found"
- ❌ 8+ touchpoints with zero engagement (no opens, no clicks)
- ❌ Breakup email sent with no response
- ✅ Then move to long-term nurture (quarterly)
