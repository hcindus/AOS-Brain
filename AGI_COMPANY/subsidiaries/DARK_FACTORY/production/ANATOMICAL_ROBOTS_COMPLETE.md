### Joint System

All robots feature:
- **Neck:** 2 DOF (pitch/yaw) - ±30°/±60°
- **Shoulders:** 3 DOF per arm
- **Elbows:** 1 DOF (hinge)
- **Wrists:** 2 DOF
- **Hands:** 15+ joints (5 fingers × 3 joints)
- **Hips:** 3 DOF per leg
- **Knees:** 1 DOF
- **Ankles:** 2 DOF

**Total DOF:** 40+ degrees of freedom

## 🔬 ANATOMICAL ACCURACY

### Based On:

1. **Netter's Atlas of Human Anatomy**
2. **Gray's Anatomy** (classic text)
3. **Visible Human Project** (NIH)
4. **Biomechanics research papers**

### Modifications for Robotics:

- Joints use servos instead of muscles/tendons
- Bones are hollow for weight reduction
- Internal channels for wiring
- Modular assembly (not fused like human bones)

### Educational Value:

- Learn human anatomy by building it
- Understand joint mechanics
- Study biomechanics
- Teach robotics + anatomy together

Most DIY robots are blocky, toy-like, or industrial. We wanted to create robots that:

Robots that blur the line between machine and humanoid. Not toy robots. Not industrial arms. Something... else.
