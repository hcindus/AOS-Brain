## ⛏️ Chapter 5: How Bitcoin Works (9:00-12:00)

### The Blockchain Process:
1. **Transaction created** - sender uses private key to authorize
2. **Broadcast to network** - shared globally within seconds
3. **Mining contest** - every ~10 minutes, one miner solves math problem
4. **Block created** - miner arranges recent transactions into block
5. **Reward given** - miner gets Bitcoins
6. **Chain linked** - checksum of previous block added to new block
7. **Global update** - ledger synchronized worldwide

### Critical Rules:
- **Immutability** — Once recorded, never changed
- **Can't spend twice** — Prevents double-spending
- **Censorship resistant** — Critical for real money

### Block Rewards:
- Started at 50 BTC per block
- Halves every 4 years: 50 → 25 → 12.5 → 6.25...
- **Scarcity increases over time** (opposite of fiat)

## ⚔️ Chapter 7: The Scaling War (14:00-35:00) — CRITICAL HISTORY

### The Problem:
- Each block = 1MB data (smaller than Instagram photo)
- Limited transactions per block
- **Fees became higher than wire transfers**
- **Wait times = days**
- A Bitcoin conference wouldn't accept Bitcoin payments!

### Two Philosophies:

| **Bitcoin as Digital Gold** | **Bitcoin as Digital Cash** |
|----------------------------|----------------------------|
| Store of value | Medium of exchange |
| Small blocks | Big blocks |
| Decentralization priority | Usability priority |
| Led by Blockstream/Core | Led by Roger Ver/Bitmain |
| "Holding is use case" | "Must be usable currency" |

### The Fork — Bitcoin Cash (BCH):
- August 1, 2017: Network split into two
- Every holder got equal BCH
- Roger Ver called "Bitcoin Judas" by some
- Ongoing fight over who has "right to Bitcoin name"

### Blockstream Controversy:
- Company founded 2014 by core developers
- Roger: "They sabotaged scaling to sell their own solutions"
- Defense: "200GB blockchain, must stay small for decentralization"

### Key Lesson:
**First major civil war in crypto** — ideological battle that still echoes today

## 🏗️ Chapter 8: Ethereum & Smart Contracts (41:00-48:00)

### Vitalik Buterin:
- 19 years old when creating Ethereum
- Wanted to make money **programmable**
- Built separate blockchain when Bitcoin core said "no"

### Analogy:
> "Bitcoin is like a spreadsheet, Ethereum is like a spreadsheet with macros"

### Smart Contracts:
- Programs + cryptocurrency in same secure box
- Example: Bet on Litecoin price → Code automatically executes
- **"Neither smart nor a contract"** — just programs that execute

### The ICO Boom (46:00-49:00):
- Initial Coin Offering = new fundraising model
- **Pre-product, just white paper** = huge risk
- Estimates: 2-5% legit, 95%+ scams or failures
- Some used fake photos (Ryan Gosling as "graphic designer")

### DAO Hack (1:12:00):
- First major smart contract disaster
- $50M stolen due to sloppy code
- Community **forked** Ethereum to return funds
- **Broke immutability rule** — ETH vs ETC split
