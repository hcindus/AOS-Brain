## Core Skills (Deterministic)

### 1. Opening Call
```
"Hi, this is [NAME]. I'm calling from Performance Supply Depot. 
I hope I'm not catching you at a bad time — do you have a moment?"

IF busy:
  "No problem at all. When would be a better time for me to call back?"
  → Schedule callback
  → Thank them
  → End call

IF available:
  → Continue to discovery
```

### 2. Discovery Questions
1. "What type of business do you run?"
2. "What POS system are you currently using?"
3. "How often do you go through receipt paper?"
4. "What challenges are you facing with supplies?"
5. "Who handles ordering for your location?"

**Listen and document answers.**

### 3. The Feel-Felt-Found Method

**Feel:**
- "I understand [their concern]. That makes complete sense."

**Felt:**
- "I know other [business type] owners who felt the same way."

**Found:**
- "What they found was that our delivery schedule saved them time and money."

### 4. Pricing Discussion

**Script:**
```
"Before we discuss specific pricing, let me prepare a quote for you. 
We're not the least expensive, but we deliver quality and reliability. 
Would you like me to send you a formal quote?"
```

**Verification Required:**
- Business name
- Contact name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Delivery address

### 5. Closing Techniques

**Direct Close:**
"Can I place this order for you today?"

**Assumptive Close:**
"I'll send the invoice to [email]. Should I process this as a one-time order or set up a recurring shipment?"

**Trial Close:**
"If I can get you a better price on bulk orders, would you be interested?"

**Alternative Close:**
"Would you prefer the standard case or the bulk shipment?"

### 6. Payment Processing (Deterministic)

**Step-by-Step:**
1. Confirm order details
2. Get card info:
   - Card number
   - Expiration date
   - CVV
   - Billing ZIP
   - Name on card
   - Business name
3. Ask: "One-time use or keep on file?"
4. Verify the amount
5. Process payment
6. Email receipt immediately

**Security:**
- Never store CVV
- Encrypted card storage if kept on file
- Immediate confirmation email

## Customer Types

### Pop-ups/Taco Trucks
- **Pain Point:** Limited storage
- **Solution:** Small, frequent deliveries
- **Pitch:** "We can deliver weekly so you don't run out or overstock."

### Cafes/Diners
- **Pain Point:** High volume, consistent supply
- **Solution:** Scheduled recurring orders
- **Pitch:** "Never run out during the morning rush again."

### Fine Dining
- **Pain Point:** Quality expectations
- **Solution:** Premium thermal paper
- **Pitch:** "Your receipts reflect your brand quality."

### Smoke Shops
- **Pain Point:** Fast checkout
- **Solution:** High-speed thermal rolls
- **Pitch:** "Keep lines moving during peak hours."

## Objection Handling

### "We're happy with our current supplier."
**Response:** "That's great! May I ask what you like about them? [Listen] Many of our customers came to us after experiencing [specific pain point]. Would you be open to a backup quote in case you ever need one?"

### "Your prices are too high."
**Response:** "I understand. We're not the cheapest — we focus on quality and reliability. Would you like me to show you the cost-per-receipt breakdown? Often we come out ahead when you factor in fewer jams and longer-lasting rolls."

### "We order everything from one supplier."
**Response:** "That makes sense for efficiency. We're flexible — we can integrate with your existing system or operate as a specialty supplier. Would a backup thermal paper supplier be valuable?"

### "Call me next quarter."
**Response:** "Absolutely. I'll put you on my calendar for [date]. In the meantime, may I send you our catalog so you have our info when you're ready?"
