## 1. BLACK WALNUT HULL (Juglans nigra)

### Active Compounds
- **Juglone** (5-hydroxy-1,4-naphthoquinone)
- **Tannins** (ellagitannins, gallotannins)
- **Iodine** content

### Research Findings
| Study | Finding | Source Type |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Traditional Use | Used for intestinal worms for centuries | Historical/Ethnobotanical |
| In Vitro Studies | Juglone shows antimicrobial activity | Laboratory research |
| Safety Profile | Generally safe in traditional doses; high doses may be toxic | Review articles |

### Mechanism of Action
- Juglone inhibits parasite metabolism
- Tannins create hostile environment for parasites
- Iodine content may support thyroid during cleanse

### Evidence Level: MODERATE
- Strong traditional use
- Limited modern clinical trials
- Primarily in vitro and animal studies

## 2. WORMWOOD (Artemisia absinthium)

### Active Compounds
- **Thujone** (neurotoxic in high doses)
- **Artemisinin** (also in Artemisia annua)
- **Sesquiterpene lactones**

### Research Findings
| Study | Finding | Source |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Artemisinin derivatives | WHO-approved for malaria | Clinical trials |
| Anthelmintic properties | Effective against roundworms | Laboratory studies |
| Broad-spectrum | Activity against various parasites | In vitro studies |

### Important Safety Notes
⚠️ **Contains thujone - neurotoxic in high doses**  
⚠️ **Not recommended for:**
- Pregnant women (can cause miscarriage)
- People with epilepsy/seizure disorders
- Long-term use (>4 weeks typically)

### Evidence Level: MODERATE-HIGH
- Artemisinin well-studied for malaria
- Traditional anthelmintic use documented
- Clinical safety concerns with thujone

## 3. CLOVES (Syzygium aromaticum)

### Active Compounds
- **Eugenol** (70-90% of oil)
- **Acetyl eugenol**
- **Beta-caryophyllene**

### Research Findings
| Study | Finding | Evidence |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Egg viability | Eugenol inhibits parasite egg development | Laboratory |
| Larval stages | Effective against larval parasites | In vitro |
| Adult parasites | Moderate effectiveness | Limited studies |

### Key Benefit
- Targets **parasite eggs** that other herbs miss
- Essential for complete cleanse (prevents reinfection)

### Evidence Level: LOW-MODERATE
- Strong laboratory evidence
- Limited human clinical trials
- Traditional use well-documented

## 4. PUMPKIN SEEDS (Cucurbita pepo)

### Active Compounds
- **Cucurbitacin** (bitter principle)
- **Zinc** (high concentration)
- **Phytosterols**

### Research Findings
| Study | Finding | Type |
|-------|---------|------|
| Cucurbitacin | Paralyzes parasites (doesn't kill) | Laboratory |
| Traditional Chinese Medicine | Used for tapeworms | Historical |
| Human studies | Some efficacy in intestinal worms | Limited clinical |

### Mechanism
- Paralyzes parasites so they detach from intestinal walls
- Must be followed by laxative or cleansing to expel
- Zinc supports immune function

### Evidence Level: MODERATE
- Mechanism understood
- Some clinical evidence
- Safe for general consumption

## 7. IVERMECTIN

### Regulatory Status
- **FDA-approved** for certain parasitic infections
- **Prescription required** in most countries
- **WHO Essential Medicine** for some tropical diseases

### Research Findings
| Condition | Evidence | Status |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Onchocerciasis | Highly effective | Gold standard |
| Strongyloidiasis | Highly effective | Gold standard |
| Scabies | Effective | First-line treatment |
| COVID-19 | Controversial/inconclusive | Not approved |

### Safety Profile
- Generally safe at prescribed doses
- Some CNS effects in high doses
- Drug interactions possible
- Requires medical supervision

### Evidence Level: HIGH
- Extensive clinical trials
- WHO-approved
- Well-established safety profile

## LIMITATIONS OF CURRENT RESEARCH

1. **Most studies are in vitro or animal** - limited human clinical trials
2. **Specific parasite types vary** - effectiveness depends on parasite species
3. **Combination studies rare** - most research on single herbs
4. **Standardization issues** - herbal preparations vary in potency
5. **Long-term safety data** - limited for extended cleanse protocols
