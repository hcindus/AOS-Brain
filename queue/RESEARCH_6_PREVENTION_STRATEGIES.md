## DAILY PREVENTION HABITS

### 1. FOOD SAFETY PROTOCOLS

#### Meat Preparation
**Temperature Guidelines:**
- **Beef, pork, lamb:** 145°F (63°C) minimum
- **Ground meats:** 160°F (71°C)
- **Poultry:** 165°F (74°C)
- **Fish:** 145°F (63°C)
- **Sushi/sashimi:** Flash freeze at -4°F (-20°C) for 7 days

**Best Practices:**
- [ ] Use meat thermometer
- [ ] Avoid cross-contamination (separate cutting boards)
- [ ] Wash hands after handling raw meat
- [ ] Freeze meat for 3-7 days before cooking (kills some parasites)
- [ ] Avoid undercooked pork (trichinosis risk)
- [ ] Avoid wild game unless properly prepared

#### Produce Washing
**For All Fruits/Vegetables:**
1. **Remove** outer leaves of lettuce/cabbage
2. **Rinse** under running water (no soap needed)
3. **Scrub** firm produce with clean brush
4. **Dry** with clean towel or paper towels
5. **Peel** when possible

**For Organic Produce:**
- Still wash thoroughly (organic ≠ parasite-free)
- Consider produce wash for extra protection

**Never Consume:**
- ❌ Unwashed produce from unknown sources
- ❌ Pre-cut produce that's been sitting out
- ❌ Sprouts (high risk - cook thoroughly)

### 2. WATER SAFETY

#### Drinking Water
**Safe Sources:**
- [ ] Municipal tap water (treated)
- [ ] Properly filtered water (NSF-certified)
- [ ] Bottled water from reputable brands
- [ ] Boiled water (rolling boil for 1 minute)

**At-Risk Situations:**
- Camping/hiking (stream/river water)
- International travel (developing countries)
- Well water (untested)
- After natural disasters

**Purification Methods:**
| Method | Effectiveness | Best For |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| **Boiling** | 100% | Camping, emergencies |
| **Filtration** (0.1 micron) | 99%+ | Daily use, travel |
| **UV Sterilization** | 99%+ | Clear water, travel |
| **Iodine tablets** | 90-95% | Emergency backup |
| **Chlorine/bleach** | 90-95% | Emergency only |

**Travel Water Protocol:**
- [ ] Drink only sealed bottled water abroad
- [ ] No ice in drinks (unless from purified water)
- [ ] Brush teeth with bottled water
- [ ] Avoid fresh juices, salads
- [ ] Check bottle seals (refilling is common)

### 3. HYGIENE PRACTICES

#### Handwashing (Critical!)
**When to Wash:**
- [ ] After using bathroom
- [ ] Before eating
- [ ] After handling animals
- [ ] After gardening/yard work
- [ ] After changing diapers
- [ ] After handling money
- [ ] After touching door handles in public

**Proper Technique:**
1. Wet hands with clean water
2. Apply soap (any kind works)
3. Scrub for 20 seconds (sing "Happy Birthday" twice)
4. Scrub all surfaces: backs, between fingers, under nails
5. Rinse thoroughly
6. Dry with clean towel or air dry

**Hand Sanitizer:**
- 60%+ alcohol content
- Use when soap not available
- Not as effective for parasites as handwashing

### 4. PET PROTOCOLS

#### Dogs
- [ ] Regular deworming (vet-prescribed)
- [ ] Pick up feces immediately (within 24 hours)
- [ ] Wash hands after handling
- [ ] Don't let dogs lick face/hands
- [ ] Keep off kitchen surfaces
- [ ] Don't feed raw meat (unless properly prepared)

#### Cats
- [ ] Indoor cats = lower risk
- [ ] Litter box daily cleaning
- [ ] Wear gloves when cleaning
- [ ] Pregnant women: avoid litter box (toxoplasmosis)

#### General
- [ ] Regular vet checkups
- [ ] Fecal tests annually
- [ ] Keep pets off beds/counters
- [ ] Wash pet bedding regularly

---

### 5. GARDENING & OUTDOOR ACTIVITIES

#### Soil Precautions
**Parasites in Soil:**
- Hookworm larvae
- Roundworm eggs (from animal feces)
- Toxoplasma oocysts (from cat feces)

**Protective Measures:**
- [ ] Wear gloves when gardening
- [ ] Wear shoes/boots (no bare feet)
- [ ] Wash hands thoroughly after
- [ ] Wash all garden produce
- [ ] Don't compost pet waste
- [ ] Cover sandboxes when not in use

#### Beach/Sand Precautions
- [ ] Wear flip-flops on beaches
- [ ] Shower after beach visits
- [ ] Avoid sand in mouth (kids especially)
- [ ] Be cautious in areas with stray animals

### 6. TRAVEL PREVENTION

#### High-Risk Destinations
**Developing Countries with Higher Parasite Rates:**
- Sub-Saharan Africa
- Southeast Asia
- Central/South America
- Parts of Middle East
- Rural India

#### Pre-Travel Prep
- [ ] Consult travel medicine doctor 4-6 weeks before
- [ ] Get travel insurance
- [ ] Pack water purification tablets
- [ ] Bring hand sanitizer
- [ ] Pack anti-diarrheal medication
- [ ] Know local medical facilities

#### Food Rules Abroad
**The "Boil It, Cook It, Peel It, or Forget It" Rule**

✅ **Safe:**
- Hot, steaming food
- Fruits you peel yourself (bananas, oranges)
- Sealed bottled drinks
- Hot coffee/tea

❌ **Avoid:**
- Street vendor food (usually)
- Raw vegetables/salads
- Unpeeled fruits
- Tap water
- Ice
- Unpasteurized dairy
- Raw/undercooked meat/seafood

#### Post-Travel
- [ ] Monitor for symptoms for 2-4 weeks
- [ ] Get tested if symptoms develop
- [ ] Consider prophylactic treatment if high exposure

## LONG-TERM IMMUNE SUPPORT

### Diet for Parasite Resistance

**Include Regularly:**
| Food Category | Examples | Benefit |
|---------------|----------|---------|
| **Probiotic foods** | Kimchi, sauerkraut, kefir, yogurt | Healthy gut flora |
| **Garlic** | Fresh, raw | Allicin content |
| **Ginger** | Fresh, tea | Digestive support |
| **Coconut oil** | Virgin, cold-pressed | Lauric acid |
| **Pumpkin seeds** | Raw | Zinc, cucurbitacin |
| **Papaya** | Ripe fruit + seeds | Papain enzyme |
| **Turmeric** | Fresh or powder | Anti-inflammatory |
| **Oregano** | Fresh or oil | Antimicrobial |

**Limit/Reduce:**
- ❌ Excessive sugar (feeds parasites)
- ❌ Excessive alcohol (weakens immunity)
- ❌ Processed foods
- ❌ Unnecessary antibiotics

## HIGH-RISK ACTIVITIES - EXTRA PRECAUTIONS

### Sushi/Raw Fish Consumption
**Risk:** Anisakis, Diphyllobothrium, other fish parasites

**Reduce Risk:**
- [ ] Choose reputable restaurants
- [ ] "Sushi-grade" = frozen at -4°F for 7 days (kills parasites)
- [ ] Avoid freshwater fish sushi
- [ ] Limit frequency (not daily)
- [ ] Avoid if immunocompromised

### Swimming in Freshwater
**Risk:** Schistosomiasis, Giardia, other water parasites

**Precautions:**
- [ ] Avoid swimming in endemic areas
- [ ] Don't swallow water
- [ ] Towel dry immediately
- [ ] Avoid swimming after rain (runoff)

### Walking Barefoot
**Risk:** Hookworm, Strongyloides

**Always Wear:**
- Shoes in developing countries
- Shoes on beaches in tropical areas
- Shoes in parks with stray animals

## EMERGENCY PREVENTION

### Suspected Exposure - Immediate Actions

**If you think you've been exposed:**
1. **Don't panic** - Most exposures don't lead to infection
2. **Document** what/when/where
3. **Monitor** for symptoms (2 days to 2 weeks)
4. **Boost** immune system immediately
5. **Consider** prophylactic herbs (garlic, pumpkin seeds)

**When to Seek Medical Care:**
- Severe symptoms develop
- High-risk exposure (known contaminated source)
- Immunocompromised
- Pregnant
