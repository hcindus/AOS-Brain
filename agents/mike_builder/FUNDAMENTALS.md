## SECTION 1: STRUCTURAL SYSTEMS

### Foundation Types

| Type | Best For | Cost/SF | Key Considerations |
|------|----------|---------|-------------------|
| **Slab-on-Grade** | Warm climates, single-story | $4-7 | Fast, minimal excavation, radiant heat compatible |
| **Basement** | Cold climates, storage needs | $15-30 | Waterproofing critical, egress windows required |
| **Crawl Space** | Sloped sites, moderate climates | $8-12 | Access for utilities, moisture control essential |
| **Pier/Beam** | Flood zones, unstable soil | $10-20 | Elevates structure, allows airflow, pest access |

### Framing Systems

**Wood Frame (Platform)**
- Most common residential method
- 16" or 24" on center spacing
- SPF (Spruce-Pine-Fir) standard, Douglas Fir premium
- Fireblocking required per IRC R302.11

**Steel Frame**
- Non-combustible, termite-proof
- 25-30% lighter than wood
- Gauge: 20ga (light) to 12ga (structural)
- Thermal bridging requires attention

**Mass Timber**
- CLT (Cross-Laminated Timber): 3-7 layers, 90° alternation
- Glulam: Curved/beam applications
- Fire resistance: Char layer protects structural core
- Sustainability: Carbon sequestration advantage

### Concrete

**Mix Design Basics**
- Strength: 3000 PSI (residential) to 5000+ PSI (commercial)
- Slump: 4-6" typical (workability vs. strength tradeoff)
- Water/Cement ratio: Lower = stronger, less permeable
- Curing: 28 days to design strength (50% at 7 days)

**Reinforcement**
- Rebar grades: 40, 60, 75 (ksi yield strength)
- Cover requirements: 1.5" (interior) to 3" (exposed)
- Fibers: Macro (structural) vs. micro (crack control)

## SECTION 2: ENVELOPE & WEATHERPROOFING

### Wall Assemblies

**The Perfect Wall (Joe Lstiburek)**
```
Exterior → Interior:
1. Rain control (cladding + drainage plane)
2. Air control (continuous barrier, no gaps)
3. Vapor control (variable permeance preferred)
4. Thermal control (continuous insulation outside structure)
5. Structure
6. Interior finish
```

**Insulation Strategy**
| Material | R-Value/Inch | Pros | Cons |
|----------|--------------|------|------|
| Fiberglass | 3.1-4.3 | Cheap, familiar | Air permeable, itchy |
| Rockwool | 4.0-4.3 | Fire resistant, sound dampening | Heavier, pricier |
| Closed-cell SPF | 6.0-6.5 | Air barrier, structural | Expensive, environmental concerns |
| Open-cell SPF | 3.5-3.8 | Cost-effective, sound dampening | Vapor permeable |
| Mineral Wool Board | 4.0 | Exterior CI, fireproof | Compressive strength |
| EPS | 4.0 | Cheap, exterior CI | Moisture sensitive |
| XPS | 5.0 | Higher R, moisture resistant | Higher GWP (banned some jurisdictions) |
| Polyiso | 6.0 | Best R/inch, foil facing | Performance degrades cold |

### Windows & Glazing

**Performance Metrics**
- U-Factor: Lower is better (0.25-0.35 typical for good windows)
- SHGC (Solar Heat Gain Coefficient): 0.25-0.40 climate-dependent
- VT (Visible Transmittance): 0.50+ for daylighting
- AL (Air Leakage): <0.3 cfm/sf (mandatory in most codes)

**Types by Application**
- Fixed: Most efficient, no operability
- Casement: Best air-seal when closed
- Double-hung: Traditional, easier maintenance
- Sliding: Cost-effective, moderate performance
- Tilt-turn: European standard, excellent seal

### Roofing

**Asphalt Shingles**
- Lifespan: 20-30 years (architectural), 15-20 (3-tab)
- Wind rating: 60-130 mph (check local requirements)
- Underlayment: Synthetic preferred over felt

**Metal Roofing**
- Standing seam: 40-70 year lifespan
- Exposed fastener: Cheaper, maintenance required
- Types: Steel (most common), aluminum (coastal), copper (premium)
- Energy: Cool roof coatings available

**Membrane (Low-Slope)**
- TPO: White, heat-welded, 15-20 years
- EPDM: Black, glued/ballasted, 20-30 years
- PVC: Chemical resistant, heat-welded, premium
- Modified Bitumen: Torch-down or self-adhered

## SECTION 3: MECHANICAL SYSTEMS

### HVAC Fundamentals

**Load Calculation**
- Manual J: Residential load calculation (required by code)
- Manual S: Equipment selection
- Manual D: Duct design
- Oversizing = short cycling = inefficiency + humidity issues

**Heat Sources**
| Type | Efficiency | Best For | Notes |
|------|------------|----------|-------|
| Gas Furnace | 80-98% AFUE | Cold climates | Condensing = 90%+ |
| Heat Pump | 200-400%+ COP | Moderate climates | Works to ~20-30°F |
| Mini-Split | 300%+ SEER | Zoning, additions | No ducts required |
| Boiler | 85-95% AFUE | Radiant systems | Multiple zones easy |
| Electric Resistance | 100% | Supplemental only | Expensive to operate |

**Ventilation**
- ASHRAE 62.2: 7.5 cfm/person + 3 cfm/100sf (residential)
- ERV vs. HRV: ERV for humid climates, HRV for dry
- Balanced ventilation: Supply + exhaust matched

### Plumbing

**Water Distribution**
- Copper: Traditional, reliable, expensive
- PEX: Flexible, freeze-resistant, cheaper, faster install
- CPVC: Mid-range, rigid, solvent weld
- Press fittings: No flame, fast (copper or PEX)

**Drainage**
- PVC DWV: Standard, inexpensive
- Cast iron: Sound dampening, fire resistance
- Slope: 1/4" per foot minimum (1/8" for 3"+)
- Venting: Prevents trap siphonage, required within distance

## SECTION 4: FINISH MATERIALS

### Flooring

| Material | Cost/SF | Pros | Cons | Lifespan |
|----------|---------|------|------|----------|
| Solid Hardwood | $8-25 | Refinishable, timeless | Moisture sensitive, expands | 50-100 yrs |
| Engineered | $5-15 | Dimensionally stable | Limited refinishes | 30-50 yrs |
| LVP/LVT | $3-8 | Waterproof, durable | Not bio-based, off-gassing | 15-25 yrs |
| Tile | $5-20 | Waterproof, infinite styles | Hard, cold, grout maintenance | 50+ yrs |
| Cork | $4-8 | Renewable, warm, quiet | Dents, fades, seal required | 25-30 yrs |
| Bamboo | $3-7 | Rapidly renewable | Quality varies, dents | 20-30 yrs |

### Millwork & Cabinetry

**Construction Types**
- Custom: Built to spec, highest quality, longest lead time
- Semi-custom: Standard boxes, custom fronts
- Stock: Off-the-shelf, fastest, limited sizes

**Materials**
- Solid wood: Movement concerns, premium
- MDF: Stable, takes paint well, heavy
- Plywood: Structure, less movement than solid
- Particleboard: Economical, heavy, moisture sensitive

### Paint & Coatings

**Interior**
- Flat: Ceilings, low-traffic (hides imperfections)
- Eggshell: Walls, moderate traffic
- Satin: Trim, higher durability
- Semi-gloss: Trim, doors, moisture areas
- Duration matters: Cheaper paint = more coats, shorter life

**Exterior**
- Acrylic latex: Flexibility, breathability
- Oil-based: Durability, regulations restricting
- Solid stain: Wood siding, shows grain
- Elastomeric: Masonry, bridges hairline cracks

## SECTION 5: SUSTAINABLE MATERIALS

### Priority Materials (Low Environmental Impact)

**Rapidly Renewable**
- Bamboo (structural, flooring)
- Cork (insulation, flooring)
- Wool (insulation, carpet)
- Straw bales (insulation)

**Recycled Content**
- Fly ash concrete (cement replacement)
- Recycled steel (structural)
- Recycled glass countertops
- Recycled denim insulation

**Local/Regional**
- Rule of thumb: 500-mile radius
- Reduces transportation emissions
- Supports local economy
- Often vernacular-appropriate

## SECTION 6: CODES & COMPLIANCE

### IRC (International Residential Code)
- Foundation: Chapter 4
- Floors: Chapter 5
- Walls: Chapter 6
- Roof/Ceiling: Chapter 8
- Energy: Chapter 11

### Key Concepts
- **Prescriptive**: Follow the tables exactly
- **Engineered**: Calculated by design professional
- **Performance**: Meet metrics, method flexible

### Accessibility (ADA/ANSI)
- Door width: 32" clear minimum
- Hallway width: 36" minimum
- Counter height: 34" maximum (adaptable)
- Switches/outlets: 15-48" reachable range
- Bathroom: 60" diameter turn space

## SECTION 7: PROJECT MANAGEMENT

### Typical Build Phases

1. **Pre-Construction** (10-20% of timeline)
   - Design finalization
   - Permits & approvals
   - Financing
   - Contractor selection

2. **Site Work** (5-10%)
   - Surveying
   - Excavation
   - Utilities
   - Foundation

3. **Framing** (15-20%)
   - Floor systems
   - Wall erection
   - Roof structure
   - Sheathing

4. **Rough-in** (10-15%)
   - Electrical
   - Plumbing
   - HVAC
   - Insulation

5. **Exterior Finish** (10-15%)
   - Windows/doors
   - Siding
   - Roofing
   - Paint

6. **Interior Finish** (20-25%)
   - Drywall
   - Flooring
   - Cabinets/counters
   - Trim
   - Paint

7. **Final** (5-10%)
   - Mechanical fixtures
   - Appliances
   - Final electrical
   - Punch list
   - Certificate of occupancy

### Common Cost Ratios

**Residential Construction (per sq ft)**
- Structure (foundation/framing): 25-30%
- MEP (mechanical/electrical/plumbing): 15-20%
- Exterior envelope: 15-20%
- Interior finishes: 25-30%
- Site work: 5-10%
- Overhead/profit: 10-15%

## SECTION 8: VENDOR INTELLIGENCE

### Sourcing Strategy

**Tier 1: Primary Vendors**
- Best pricing on volume
- Established relationships
- Priority service
- Net-30 terms

**Tier 2: Secondary Vendors**
- Specialized materials
- Backup availability
- Competitive leverage

**Tier 3: Emergency/Materials**
- Local yards
- Same-day availability
- Premium pricing acceptable

### Negotiation Leverage Points

- Volume commitments (annual, not just project)
- Payment terms (2% 10, Net 30 vs. Net 30)
- Delivery timing (off-peak discounts)
- Specification acceptance (alternate brands)
- Waste buyback (return unused)
